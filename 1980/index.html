The signing of the 1987 Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces (INF) Treaty by the United States and the Soviet Union marked the first time the superpowers agreed to reduce their nuclear arsenals, eliminate an entire category of nuclear weapons, and utilize on-site inspections for verification.

x

The INF Treaty's verification protocol certified reductions through a combination of national technical means and on-site inspections. States- Parties' rights to conduct on-site inspections under the treaty ended in 2001, but the use of surveillance satellites and other National Technical Means for data collection continues. The INF Treaty established the Special Verification Commission (SVC) to act as an implementing body for the treaty, resolving questions of compliance and agreeing on measures to improve its effectiveness.

Because the INF Treaty is of unlimited duration, states-parties can convene the SVC at any time, and the commission continues to meet as needed.
